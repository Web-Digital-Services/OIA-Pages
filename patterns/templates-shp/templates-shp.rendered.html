School of Health Professions

Great healthcare needs allies

The School of Health Professions prepares outstanding healthcare professionals and leaders in the allied health fields. These providers may be the first responders in medical emergencies, the discerning eyes in the lab, or the physical therapists making a home visit.

UT Health provides degrees in seven allied fields: Emergency Health Sciences, Medical Laboratory Sciences, Occupational Therapy, Physical Therapy, Physician Assistant Studies, Respiratory Care, Medical Sciences and Imaging Sciences and Speech-Language Pathology.

Medical Laboratory Sciences

Medical laboratory scientists provide physicians with information to detect disease or a predisposition to disease, confirm a diagnosis, and guide patient management.

Emergency Health Sciences

Emergency Health Sciences provides paramedics an opportunity to advance their education by focusing on advanced medical care, EMS management and EMS education.

Occupational Therapy

Occupational Therapists help people whose lives have been interrupted by accidents, illnesses, or congenital conditions build motor skills and cognitive and emotional functioning.

Physical Therapy

Physical therapists work most often with people with injuries, illnesses, or disabilities who need to improve their ability to move.

Physician Assistant Studies

The Physician Assistant (PA) Studies program is one of only seven in the state of Texas and one of only two with a presence in South Texas, providing greater access to healthcare.

Respiratory Care

Working closely with physicians and nurses, respiratory therapists perform critical diagnostic and treatment procedures for patients who must fight to breathe.
